Development of an Enhanced Rating System for Test Cricket Adapting the Glicko Model

Sports Analytics Research

This project develops a novel rating system for Test cricket players by adapting the Glicko rating system, originally designed for chess. The Glicko model accounts for rating reliability through rating deviation (RD), which increases during periods of inactivity and decreases as more games are played.

The enhanced system addresses limitations of traditional cricket ratings by:

  • Incorporating rating deviation to measure confidence in player ratings
  • Accounting for the quality of opposition faced
  • Adjusting for match conditions and home/away factors
  • Providing more responsive ratings that reflect current form

Methods: Glicko-2 algorithm adaptation, Bayesian inference, historical match data analysis

Applications of Higher Order Markov Chains and Pressure Index to Strategize Controlled Run Chases in Twenty20 Cricket

Sports Analytics Research

This research applies higher-order Markov chain models to analyze and optimize batting strategies during T20 run chases. The project introduces a Pressure Index metric to quantify the situational pressure on batting teams.

Key contributions include:

  • Development of state-space models capturing ball-by-ball transitions
  • Creation of a Pressure Index incorporating required rate, wickets, and overs remaining
  • Strategic recommendations for optimal scoring rates under different pressure scenarios
  • Analysis of successful chase patterns across international T20 matches

Methods: Higher-order Markov chains, Pressure Index formulation, Monte Carlo simulation, strategic optimization

An Evaluation of IPL Player Rankings: Statistical Analysis & Predictive Modelling

Sports Analytics Research | R and Machine Learning

A comprehensive evaluation of player ranking systems in the Indian Premier League using statistical analysis and machine learning techniques. This project examines existing ranking methodologies and proposes improvements using predictive modeling.

The research encompasses:

  • Statistical analysis of historical IPL performance data
  • Comparison of existing player valuation and ranking methods
  • Development of ML-based predictive models for player performance
  • Feature engineering for cricket-specific metrics
  • Validation against actual auction prices and team selections

Methods: R programming, Random Forests, Gradient Boosting, regression analysis, feature selection
