Applications of higher order Markov models and Pressure Index to strategize controlled run chases in Twenty20 cricket

A quantitative approach to mastering T20 run chases—this work combines Markov modeling with a novel Pressure Index to decode how successful teams manage game pressure. The result is a practical, data-backed strategy for controlled and efficient chases.

  • Published in: International Journal of Sports Science and Coaching

An Augmented Rating System for Test Cricket: adapting Glicko's model

An enhanced rating system for Test cricket that adapts Glicko's model to account for home advantage, toss impact, scheduling imbalances, and margin of victory. This probabilistically grounded framework delivers fairer team rankings and stronger predictive accuracy compared to the current ICC system.

  • Collaborator: Diganta Mukherjee
  • Status: Currently under review
  • DOI: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02574

Catches Win Matches: A Statistical Analysis of Fielding Impact in T20 Cricket

A large-scale statistical analysis of fielding impact in T20 cricket using over 9,500 matches and 2.15 million deliveries. We quantify catch drop rates (21.7%) extensively at team-level and tournament-level, their effect on bowling economy and batting totals, high-pressure momentum shifts, ground hotspots, and player consistency, leading up to several interesting findings on the impact of fielding in the shortest format.

This work is an ongoing academic project intended solely for educational purposes and should not be distributed or used without appropriate attribution.
